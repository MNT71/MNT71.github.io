Benchmark 13-54: Expressing your own viewpoint

Throughout my senior year, I crafted numerous essays and paragraphs for college applications and scholarship opportunities, each requiring me to express my viewpoint. For instance, when applying to the University of Michigan, one of the required essays prompted me to reflect on the concept of community and articulate what it meant to me personally. Additionally, in a scholarship essay addressing the effects of cyberbullying, I conveyed my perspective by emphasizing the profound impact of words and how they can harm individuals if internalized. This experience exemplifies Benchmark 13-54, which emphasizes the importance of expressing one's viewpoint. Through these essays, I had the opportunity to assert my beliefs, values, and experiences, contributing to a deeper understanding of myself and the world around me. Moreover, as an aspiring engineer, the ability to articulate and advocate for my viewpoints is essential for collaborating effectively with peers, communicating ideas, and driving innovation in problem-solving processes.
