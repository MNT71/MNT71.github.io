Letters of Recommendation

I received a letter of recommendation from Mrs. Sponsler, who serves as the historian of Western High School, teaches U.S. History and APUSH, and advises the yearbook staff. Over the years, I've had the privilege of working with her in four years of Yearbook, one year of APUSH, and a semester of independent study, in addition to occasional encounters at track meets. One of my earliest memories with Mrs. Sponsler was during a Yearbook session held amidst the COVID pandemic, where everyone wore masks. Throughout high school, nobody has witnessed my growth as both a student and an individual more than her. Her letter emphasizes my willingness to explore alternative approaches, embrace learning opportunities, maintain focus, and make significant contributions, regardless of the role assigned. I received a letter of recommendation from Miss Pack, who initially served as a math and health teacher before transitioning to the role of assistant principal during our sophomore year. While I've only had the opportunity to be in her health class throughout high school, her presence has been felt as she roamed the halls, demonstrating her dedication to student's success beyond the confines of the classroom. Miss Pack has been a significant figure and support throughout my high school journey and as I pursue my college endeavors, her guidance proving invaluable in shaping my academic and personal growth. Her endorsement underscores the profound impact of her mentorship on my trajectory, affirming the depth of her investment in my development. Her letter not only emphasizes my creative disposition, commitment to learning, and dedication to supporting my peers both inside and outside the classroom but also serves as a testament to her insightful understanding of my abilities and potential.
